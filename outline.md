## Introduction

Classical mechanics as the physics you can verify against lived experience — throw a ball, watch the trajectory, check the simulation. Foundation for everything else in the series.

## Chapters

1. **What is Physics? / The Nature of Science** — D3 scale explorer (Planck length → observable universe); units, measurement, scientific method.
2. **Motion in One Dimension / Kinematics** — D3 position-velocity-acceleration live plotter; displacement, velocity, acceleration, free fall.
3. **Two-Dimensional Kinematics** — D3 projectile trajectory simulator; vector decomposition, projectile motion, independence of components.
4. **Forces and Newton's Laws** — D3 free-body diagram builder; the three laws, force as cause of acceleration.
5. **Friction, Drag, and Elasticity** — D3 terminal-velocity race; non-conservative forces, Hooke's law, drag regimes.
6. **Circular Motion and Gravitation** — D3 orbit animator; centripetal acceleration, Newton's gravitation, orbital mechanics.
7. **Work, Energy, and Energy Resources** — D3 energy bar chart through a process; work, KE, PE, conservation, power.
8. **Momentum and Collisions** — D3 1D collision simulator; impulse, conservation of momentum, elastic vs inelastic.
9. **Statics and Torque** — D3 torque-balance puzzle; equilibrium conditions, center of mass.
10. **Rotational Motion and Angular Momentum** — D3 figure-skater simulator; moment of inertia, conservation of L.
11. **Fluid Statics** — D3 pressure-depth + Archimedes; Pascal's principle, buoyancy.
12. **Fluid Dynamics** — D3 Bernoulli flow visualizer; continuity, Bernoulli, viscosity, turbulence.
13. **Waves and Their Properties** — D3 wave interference simulator; superposition, standing waves, resonance.
14. **Sound and Hearing** — D3 standing wave modes; sound as pressure wave, Doppler effect, music and instruments.
